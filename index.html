First Trimester
Weeks 1-12
  • Baby's heart begins to beat around week 6
  • Major organs begin forming
  • Morning sickness may peak
  • First ultrasound typically scheduled
Parent Tip: Start taking prenatal vitamins if you haven't already.
Second Trimester
Weeks 13-26
  • Baby's movements become noticeable
  • Gender can typically be determined
  • Anatomy scan usually performed
  • Energy levels often improve
Parent Tip: This is often the best time for travel and preparing the nursery.
Third Trimester
Weeks 27-40
  • Baby's brain develops rapidly
  • Lungs mature for breathing
  • Baby gains most of their weight
  • Braxton Hicks contractions may begin
Parent Tip: Pack your hospital bag and finalize birth plan preparations.
4th Trimester
0-3 months
  • First social smiles appear
  • Head control begins to develop
  • Sleep patterns start to emerge
  • Feeding routines establish
Parent Tip: Focus on bonding, rest when baby sleeps, and accept help from others.
Growing Baby
3-6 months
  • Rolls from tummy to back
  • Reaches for and grasps objects
  • Begins to laugh and babble
  • Shows interest in solid foods
Parent Tip: Introduce tummy time and consider starting solid foods around 6 months.
Sitting & Exploring
6-9 months
  • Sits without support
  • Transfers objects between hands
  • Begins to crawl or scoot
  • Responds to their name
Parent Tip: Baby-proof your home and encourage exploration in safe environments.
Moving & Grooving
9-12 months
  • Pulls to standing position
  • Cruises along furniture
  • Says first words
  • Plays peek-a-boo and waves
Parent Tip: Encourage standing and walking while ensuring a safe environment.
First Steps
12-15 months
  • Takes first independent steps
  • Uses simple words with meaning
  • Shows preferences for activities
  • Imitates actions and sounds
Parent Tip: Celebrate milestones and provide plenty of opportunities for practice.
Toddler Time
15-18 months
  • Walks independently and confidently
  • Vocabulary expands to 10-20 words
  • Shows independence in play
  • Begins to assert preferences
Parent Tip: Encourage independence while maintaining consistent routines.
Growing Independence
18-21 months
  • Runs and climbs stairs
  • Uses 50+ words
  • Begins parallel play
  • Shows emotional regulation
Parent Tip: Support growing independence while setting gentle boundaries.
Almost Two
21-24 months
  • Combines words into phrases
  • Shows interest in potty training
  • Engages in pretend play
  • Follows simple instructions
Parent Tip: Prepare for increased independence and communication skills.
Two and a Half
2.5 years
  • Speaks in complete sentences
  • Shows empathy for others
  • Engages in cooperative play
  • Demonstrates problem-solving skills
Parent Tip: Encourage social interactions and continue language development.
Three Years Old
3 years
  • Pedals tricycle
  • Uses complex sentences
  • Shows interest in letters and numbers
  • Plays cooperatively with others
Parent Tip: Consider preschool programs and continue fostering curiosity.
Four Years Old
4 years
  • Hops on one foot
  • Tells stories and asks many questions
  • Shows increased attention span
  • Demonstrates self-control
Parent Tip: Prepare for more complex learning and social situations.
Kindergarten Ready
5+ years
  • Demonstrates school readiness skills
  • Shows emotional and social maturity
  • Follows multi-step instructions
  • Expresses thoughts and feelings clearly
Parent Tip: Celebrate this major milestone and continue supporting their learning journey.
